Are you in the Flow?

Post a Comment

Design

So I was sitting on the subway on my way to Union station to go see a my very first raptors game ever (WE WON!). What caught my eye on the long subway ride from work was a Flow 93.5 advertisement. Now I’ve seen other variations of the ad for the past year, but this one really captivated me.

The swirly serif font that looked perfectly groovy, a reputation that definitely shined through as a hip, modern, music station.

The second feature of the ad is the interesting use of drop shadows and defragmentation of each stroke of the letter to give a “cut out” effect.

Thirdly, the overlaying coffee stained texture paints the font a “happy” golden yellow while showing glimpses of hope in the white areas of the text. Their use of black to make the typography pop is really effective and gives a subtle repetition of the drop shadow.

The fourth and final feature on this ad really appealed to me was that fact that you know that it wasn’t a rushed sloppy ad. The length of the serif in the “I” aligned perfectly with the serif of the “T”. The width of the smaller tagline fit snuggly under the full length of the word “Flow”. That sums up why I love this ad is that each element of this ad ties in with one another and completes the design as a whole.

This ad definitely has flow ; ) Well, that was cheesy.

I’m out!
