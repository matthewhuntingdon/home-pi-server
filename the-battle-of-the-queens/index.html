The Battle of the Queens

Post a Comment

Design, People & Technology, Videos

If you’ve recently traveled to downtown Toronto by public transit, you’ve probably seen this cleverly placed ad:


source: http://torontoseeker.com/item-7231872.htm

My favourite part about this ad is that Disney has molded this campaign around the a given environment blurring the lines between blatant marketing and immersive art installations. I think this type of advertising certainly makes the commuters trip a little more exciting (at least for me!) and makes the good impact. I enjoyed the fact that they also played with the direction of the trains to define the Red Queen and the White Queen. Trains going northbound being White Queens territory and southbound being the Red Queen’s, commuters are recruited into the respective Queens’ army. Fun!

Having been giving the opportunity to work downtown, I’ve been really looking at advertisements critically, particularly in the way they are presented as well the art direction. In addition to travelling downtown, my work has also allowed me to sit in on several brainstorming sessions and meetings where I’ve learned a lot of about various marketing styles and tactics. One that stood out to me was storyline marketing. I don’t know the official term for this, but let me explain: This is when a static ad is put up at public transit systems for a period of time, long enough for every-day commuters to remember the message. The ad is then followed up by another ad that relates to the first. Cleverly related. The most recent one I’ve seen is the series “Unlimited” ads I’ve been noticing on bus shelters and subway platforms. So far, the ad has been presented as the word “Unlimited” overlaying nonchalant photos of every day people.

I’m excited to see how this marketing campaign will unfold and reveals their brand!
